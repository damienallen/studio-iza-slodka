Makers' Maze
Europan 15 winning entry

Makers’ Maze is a winning entry for Europan 15 competition for Vierhavensblok in Rotterdam. The choice of this site was motivated by my ongoing fascination with postindustrial sites and informal urban spaces. The prior research and observations could be implemented in this Rotterdam harbour context.

In Makers’ Maze architecture is reconnected with the economic, social and political realities of the contemporary city by offering a flexible method rather than a rigid design. The goal was to create new conditions for creativity, circularity, interaction, sharing and freedom while preserving the existing.

The charm of the Vierhavensblok is its informal character where one finds the unexpected around each corner and where chance encounters breed new collaborations. Inspired by these qualities I proposed a series of interlocking building clusters organized around a network of passages, streets and squares, creating a unified, resilient, and intriguing urban scheme. The ambition was to build on the present sense of community and self-initiative by leaving enough room for organic growth and adaptation.

Collage & Continuity
YTAA 2016 nomination

During my graduation project I explored a post-industrial site in my home city: Kraków, Poland. There I proposed a Craft and Design School that would enhance the private and intimate nature of craft, building on the specific character of the location. The project focuses on the relation between buildings and objects, looking for the balance between the continuity of the spaces and the collage of scales, materials and colors that create them.

The site of the project is mostly hidden, introverted environment. The final design consists of buildings and series of spaces, that serve different functions, have different identities and together form “a Village” — a platform of interaction for different groups of users of the proposed school. Spaces are collections of elements and their characters are established by defining relations between all parts. These parts are juxtaposed together and create a patchwork of scales, forms, materials and colours, or the contrary: a homogeneous whole.

About

Starting already at university, I became fascinated with post-industrial sites. These temporarily forgotten areas have unique character, architecture and urban fabric. No wonder that these qualities attract makers, designers, and crafters, creating a vibrant and creative atmosphere so needed in modern cities. Quite often these activities are among the first signs of gentrification — upcoming developments have the potential to change the character of an area forever.

I am convinced that another way is possible: building on the found qualities can enable us to design truly unique and integrated in a city neighbourhoods, where their inhabitants stay and continue building community, including new users and spaces. Recently my proposal that follows these principles: Makers’ Maze for Vierhavensblok in Rotterdam was awarded 1 prize in European competition Europan15.
